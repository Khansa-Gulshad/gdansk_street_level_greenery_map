About Street-Level Analysis

This map visualizes street-level greening conditions and future planting potential for Gdańsk using street-view imagery analysis. Google Street View images were collected along the street network and analysed using deep learning–based image segmentation to quantify visible urban elements.

The Indices

Green View Index (GVI)

Measures the proportion of visible vegetation in street-view imagery. Higher values indicate more greenery visible from street level.

Impervious Area Index (IAI)

Quantifies impervious surfaces (roads, pavement) visible in street views. Higher values indicate more built-up environments.

Open Space Index (OSI)

Reflects visible open, non-built, non-vegetated areas (bare ground, terrain). Higher values indicate more open space availability.

Tree Planting Potential on Poor Streets

In addition to assessing existing street-level greenery, this map explores the potential for future tree planting on streets with low visual greenery.

For these streets, a predictive model estimates how many trees could realistically be added, considering street length, existing trees, and physical planting constraints.

Users can explore three planting scenarios based on different spacing assumptions (5 m, 10 m, and 15 m between trees).

District vs. Street Scale

At the district scale, colors represent the average street-level conditions within each district. Values are calculated as length-weighted averages, meaning longer street segments contribute more to the district value than shorter ones.
